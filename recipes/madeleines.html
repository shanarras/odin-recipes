Madeleines

Madeleines were always a staple during our Costco runs. The ones in the plastic tin were yummy and sold at good value. I wanted to make them myself to save myself gas (and money in the future heh). I went on Amazon and impulsively bought a madeleine pan. These madeleines were so easy to make that I don't think I'll be getting those store-bought madeleines any time soon.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 130g (2/3 cup) sugar
  • 7.5ml (1 1/2 tsp) orange zest
  • 120g (1 cup) flour
  • 5ml (1 tsp) baking powder
  • 1.25ml (1/4 tsp) salt
  • 60ml (6 tbsp) melted butter
  • 7g (1 tbsp) powdered sugar

Steps

  1. Beat eggs with sugar until pale yellow. Add the orange zest.
  2. Whisk together dry ingredients. Add to the egg mixture until just combined. Add melted butter.
  3. Refrigerate batter for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  4. Preheat oven to 176°C (350F°).
  5. Lightly coat a non-stick Madeleine pan with baking spray.
  6. Spoon 1 tablespoon (15ml) of batter into the center of the well and don't touch it. It will spread out by itself and keep a more uniform shape that way.
  7. Bake 7-8 mins until slightly golden around edges and centers look set and a slight hump has appeared. Allow to cool slightly and remove from the tin.
  8. Once cooled completely, dust with powdered sugar and serve.
